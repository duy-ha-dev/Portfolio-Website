Do Monte Lab

Within the Do Monte Lab, I specialize in the computational analysis of neural data to understand emotional memory formation. I utilize machine learning techniques, such as the CEBRA, to compress and analyze complex time-series neural activity. This reveals critical patterns associated with emotional memory circuits. Additionally, I conduct computational analyses of prefrontal cortex neuron activity during reward and fear behaviors. I am assisting to uncover how the brain processes these stimuli, offering potential insights for the development of anxiety and substance abuse disorder treatments.
