(GENIE) Genomic Expression Navigation and Integration Engine: A Genomic Read Mapper

Read mapping is a fundamental task in computational biology, relevant to reference-guided assembly, variant calling, and gene expression analysis. It became popularized in 2003 through the Human Genome Project, which studied high-throughput mapping methods, costing about $3 billion over 13 years. Read mapping involves aligning huge numbers of very short DNA sequences, or reads, to a reference genome. This is a challenging task because the reads are often incomplete and may contain errors. After preprocessing and alignment, reads must be mapped back to a reference genome to determine their origin. Our project aims to develop a cost-effective and time-efficient mapping method that accurately maps reads to complete gene expressions.

Addressing these challenges, we introduce GENIE (Genomic Expression Navigation and Integration Engine), a Python package designed to revolutionize the process of read mapping. GENIE offers a systematic approach to perform precise mapping of genomic reads, a cornerstone task in computational biology. GENIE is tailored to provide a cost-effective and time-efficient solution, enabling precise mapping of reads to complete gene expressions. By leveraging advanced algorithms and user-friendly interfaces, GENIE aims to simplify and enhance the accuracy of read mapping, making it a valuable tool in the arsenal of computational biology and genomic research.
